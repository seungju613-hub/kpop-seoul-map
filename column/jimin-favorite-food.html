What Does Jimin Actually Eat? A Korean ARMY Breaks Down His Favorite Foods

✍️ By @bomnalcafe · 2026-04-01

If you've ever wondered what BTS's Jimin eats on a regular day, the answer might surprise you — because it's incredibly simple.

No fancy restaurants. No exotic ingredients. No Instagram-worthy food trends. Jimin's taste in food is what Koreans affectionately call 초딩입맛 (choeding ipmat) — literally, "elementary schooler's palate." It means he loves the kind of food that Korean kids grow up eating: kimchi fried rice, ramyeon, mandu (dumplings), tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes).

And honestly? That's part of why Korean fans find it so endearing.

Jimin's Food List — Surprisingly Basic

Here's what Jimin is known to love:

🍚 Kimchi — the ultimate Korean staple
🍚 Kongnamul-gukbap — bean sprout rice soup (a classic hangover cure)
🥩 Meat — samgyeopsal (pork belly), galbi (ribs), all the grilled things
🌶️ Tteokbokki — spicy rice cakes
🍳 Kimchi fried rice — comfort food royalty
🍜 Ramyeon — especially Buldak (fire noodles!)
🥟 Mandu — Korean dumplings
🍋 Lemon soju — his signature drink

BTS on Run BTS — Jimin and the members get serious about kimchi.

What's interesting here is that every single item on this list is what Koreans call 분식 (bunsik) — affordable, everyday street food and home-cooked meals. These are the dishes you can find at literally every corner in Korea. Nothing on Jimin's list requires a reservation or a dress code.

Jimin himself has admitted that he's not a picky eater. He's even said he enjoys the kimbap and snacks sold at Korean convenience stores. For context, that's like saying your favorite meal is a gas station sandwich — except Korean convenience store food is actually really good. (Seriously, if you ever visit Korea, don't sleep on the convenience stores.)

The Busan Plot Twist

Here's the part that confuses Koreans.

Jimin is from Busan — Korea's second-largest city, located right on the southeastern coast. Busan is famous for its seafood. The fish markets, the raw fish restaurants, the fresh sashimi — Busan people are known for growing up surrounded by incredible ocean food.

But Jimin? He doesn't really like seafood.

A Busan boy who avoids fish. Korean fans find this hilarious and oddly charming. It's like being from Naples and not liking pizza. It shouldn't work, but somehow it's very Jimin — doing things his own way, quietly and without explanation.

The Spicy + Sour Combo

One thing that does match the Korean stereotype: Jimin loves spicy food.

He's a known fan of Buldak Bokkeum-myeon (불닭볶음면) — Samyang's infamous fire noodles that have become a global sensation through YouTube challenges. While most people struggle through a bowl, Jimin genuinely enjoys them.

Jimin happily eating Buldak fire noodles — this is not a challenge for him, it's just lunch.

And speaking of Buldak — it seems to be a BTS-wide obsession at this point. Jungkook famously invented his own Buldak makguksu noodle recipe that went viral. If you haven't tried it yet, we have the full recipe right here on our site.

But here's the less common part: Jimin also loves sour food. Enjoying spicy food is very Korean. Enjoying sour food? That's actually a bit unusual here. Most Koreans prefer sweet or savory over sour.

Jimin's Lemon Soju

Jimin is known to enjoy soju — Korea's most iconic alcoholic drink. His reported tolerance is about one bottle, which is roughly 360ml at around 17-18% alcohol. That's a moderate amount by Korean drinking standards.

Jimin enjoying drinks — his go-to is soju with a twist of lemon.

His signature twist: adding lemon juice to soju. This gives the already sharp-tasting spirit an extra sour kick.

Now, full transparency — I tried Jimin's lemon soju method myself. Soju is already a strong, clean-tasting spirit, and adding lemon juice basically turns it into... well, imagine vodka mixed with vinegar. I'll be honest: it was not my thing. At all. 😂

But that's Jimin's palate — he genuinely enjoys that sharp, sour punch. If you're the kind of person who bites into lemons without flinching, this might be your drink.

Kimchi Fried Rice — Easy Recipe

Since kimchi fried rice is one of Jimin's favorites (and honestly, one of Korea's greatest comfort foods), here's a simple recipe you can make at home.

Kimchi fried rice topped with a sunny-side-up egg — Jimin's ultimate comfort food.
BTS making kimchi fried rice on a variety show — chaotic, delicious, very Bangtan.

Bacon Kimchi Fried Rice (베이컨 김치볶음밥)

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 400g cooked rice (about 2 cups — day-old rice works best)
  • 70–100g bacon, chopped (substitute with Spam, canned tuna, or pork)
  • 180g kimchi, roughly chopped
  • 20g sugar (about 1.5 tablespoons)
  • 10g gochugaru / Korean chili flakes (optional)
  • 20g dashida / Korean beef stock powder (optional)
  • 2 eggs

How to make it:

1. Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Fry the bacon first until it starts to get crispy and releases its fat — this becomes your flavor base.

2. Add the chopped kimchi and stir-fry together with the bacon for about 2 minutes. The kimchi should start to caramelize slightly — that's where the magic happens.

3. Add the rice and mix everything together thoroughly. You want each grain of rice coated in the oil and kimchi juices.

4. Add the sugar, gochugaru, and dashida all at once. Stir-fry for another minute or two until everything is well incorporated.

5. In a separate pan, fry two eggs sunny-side up — whites fully cooked, yolks still runny. This is non-negotiable.

6. Place the fried rice in a bowl, top with the egg, and when you're ready to eat — break the yolk. Let it flow over the rice. That first bite? That's what Jimin is eating on his days off.

What I love about Jimin's food taste is how Korean it is in the most ordinary way. He doesn't chase trends. He doesn't need expensive ingredients. He just wants kimchi, rice, some meat, and a cold bottle of soju with lemon.

And in a world where celebrities post $200 omakase dinners, there's something deeply comforting about a global superstar whose ultimate comfort food is convenience store kimbap.

Just... don't say I didn't warn you about that lemon soju. 💜
